When the Ink Dries

Writing can be a hard worn passion that encapsulates driven anger. While it can allow the wings of a butterfly to morph into lengths and colors unknown. A third metamorphosis that doesn't exist and yet is right there in front of you. Staring you in the eyes with a life of mystery not yet spoken as you haven't yet turned the page. Without writing, I am nothing but a naive wander lusting child hoping to be something great.

When the Passion Ignites

Iconic white letters imprinted on round black keys, the black metal of the apparatus almost sparkles in the dull luminescent lighting of the open spaced room, the sleek platen does its rightful duty, keeping a stronghold on the crisp piece of paper scrolled inside. It was the partner in every crime, painting syllables into every twist and turn, splattering intrigue across the pages. Waiting ever so patiently for the next who may come upon it.

The typewriter's allure called her in, the peculiar elevation to each row of keys trails a stairway of letters. One at a time, the coolness meets her small fingertips, dancing up towards the paper as if the hand had magically become a person only to satisfy this need to walk them, the tiles sending signals to her bones, urging something. Too young to grasp what it is calling to her, only knowing the pleasant smoothness of the rimmed keys in the shape of finger pads of a much larger person than she.

It feels as though she is matching the call, responding in agreement. She was pleasantly enthused when she felt no resounding footsteps in her direction, no stern hand on her shoulder to pull her out of her stupor and guide her away, no ushered words reaching her ears in a scolding tone. Her little fingers play at the rims in a hesitant manner, deliberating what it is she should paint her pages with. It was magic, a creation upon creation that stabilized roots for the next creators who hoped to bring their art to life.

Not every spark of creativity starts the same, nor does it always lead down the same path. The first time I felt the thrill of possibilities, I didn't know where it would take me. And despite the time that has passed me by, I'm still standing on the same road, wondering where it'll lead me to next. When I look through my camera, or place my hands on the keys of my laptop, I'm reminded of that wide eyed child.
